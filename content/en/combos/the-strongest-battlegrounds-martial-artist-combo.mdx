### Basic Combo Starter

This combo focuses on initiating with Bullet Barrage and following up with reliable damage.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initiate with Bullet Barrage">
    Start your assault with **Bullet Barrage**. This move is excellent for catching opponents and setting up subsequent attacks. If their ragdoll is cancelled, it allows for any follow-up.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Follow with Whirlwind Drop">
    After Bullet Barrage, immediately use **Whirlwind Drop**. This is a safer option if you suspect the opponent might still have their ragdoll evade. It minimizes risk compared to other moves.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Extend with Head First (if evade is down)">
    If your enemy has already used their evade dash, follow up with **Head First**. This move stuns them again, allowing your other abilities to cool down and extending your combo window.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Finish with Vanishing Kick (Used Twice)">
    Conclude the sequence with **Vanishing Kick (Used Twice)**. This move is a fantastic combo extender and can deal significant damage, especially with its second hit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Evade Dash Awareness">
Always be mindful of your opponent's evade dash. Using high-commitment moves like Head First when their evade is active can leave you vulnerable to counter-attacks. Baiting their evade before committing to these moves is crucial.
</Callout>

### Awakened Combo Example (StormyRae15's 100% Combo)

This combo utilizes the Martial Artist's awakened state to achieve maximum damage, potentially leading to a one-shot.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initiate with Front Dash + 3 M1s">
    Start with a quick **Front Dash** to close distance, followed by three basic attacks (**3 M1s**) to initiate the combo and gain meter.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Activate Twin Fangs">
    Immediately follow with **Twin Fangs**. This move is a powerful combo extender in its awakened state, dealing significant damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Unleash Ground Fissure">
    After Twin Fangs, use **Ground Fissure**. This move grants temporary invincibility frames (i-frames) and further extends the combo, preventing the opponent from escaping.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Finish with Last Breath">
    Conclude the combo with **Last Breath**. This is Suiryu's strongest ultimate, boasting a massive hitbox and immense damage, often capable of finishing off an opponent instantly if executed correctly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Strategic Ultimate Use">
**Last Breath** is most effective as a finisher due to its high damage and uncounterable nature. **Twin Fangs** is better for extending combos, but be wary of its counterability if used randomly. **Earth Splitting Strike** offers range but also carries counter risk.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Counterplay and Defensive Strategies">
Even the strongest battlegrounds Martial Artist combo can be nullified with proper counterplay. Understanding how to defend against Suiryu and how to adapt your combos to enemy defenses is vital.

### Common Counterplay Tactics

| Defensive Action | Martial Artist Move Affected | Counter Strategy                                                                        |
| :--------------- | :--------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Evade Dash**   | Bullet Barrage, Head First   | Bait out the evade dash first, then commit to these moves.                               |
| **Counter**      | Vanishing Kick (Used Twice), Twin Fangs, Earth Splitting Strike | Delay the second hit of Vanishing Kick, or only use counterable ultimate moves after baiting enemy counter. |
| **Ragdoll Evade**| Bullet Barrage, Whirlwind Drop | Use Whirlwind Drop as a safer follow-up to Bullet Barrage if ragdoll evade is active.    |

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Baiting Evades">
    One of the most effective strategies against skilled opponents is to **bait out their evade dash**. Use a quick, less committal attack or even a few M1s to provoke an evade, then unleash your full combo once they are vulnerable.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Knowing When to Disengage">
    If your combo is broken or your opponent manages to evade, sometimes the best strategy is to **disengage and reset**. Use moves like the passive mini-uppercut followed by Vanishing Kick to create distance and reposition for another attack.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning" title="Predicting Opponent Movement">
Whirlwind Drop is a predictive move. If you anticipate an opponent dashing towards you, a well-timed Whirlwind Drop can catch them off guard and lead into a new combo. Conversely, if you use it randomly, they can easily punish you.
</Callout>
