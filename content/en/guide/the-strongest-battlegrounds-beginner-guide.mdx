At its core, combat revolves around your basic attacks, known as **M1s** (Mouse Button 1). These are your primary method for initiating and extending combos. Beyond simple M1s, the game introduces variations that allow for more strategic play and combo setups.

<Callout type="tip" title="Mastering M1 Combos">
Practice the different M1 combo variations in a private server. The **3M1 + Uppercut** and **3M1 + Slam** are vital for setting up advanced combos and controlling your opponent's position.
</Callout>

### Basic Attack Types

| Attack Type      | Input (PC)                       | Description                                                                                             | Primary Use                                     |
| :--------------- | :------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | :---------------------------------------------- |
| **Normal Combo** | Hold Left Mouse Button           | A sequence of up to four hits that ragdolls the opponent on the final strike.                         | Basic damage, combo initiation.                 |
| **Uppercut Combo** | Hold Left Mouse Button + Jump    | The final hit becomes an uppercut, launching the opponent into the air.                                 | Air combos, positional advantage.               |
| **Slam Combo**   | 3 M1s, then Tap Jump + Attack      | After three hits, a timed jump and attack result in a powerful slam.                                    | Grounding opponents, heavy damage, setup.       |

### Blocking and Critical Hits

Blocking is a defensive mechanic that can mitigate incoming damage. By holding **F**, you can guard against basic attacks and some skills from a 180-degree angle. However, blocking slows your movement and leaves you vulnerable to certain block-breaking attacks.

<Callout type="warning" title="Block Vulnerability">
Your back is vulnerable while blocking. Opponents can use forward dashes or quick movement to circle around and hit you from behind, bypassing your guard.
</Callout>

Perfectly timing a block against an opponent's M1 can lead to a **Critical Hit**. This enhances your next basic attack, tripling its damage and setting the stage for more advanced mechanics like the **Black Flash**. Critical Hits can be chained, rewarding precise defensive play.

### The Power of Black Flash

A **Black Flash** is an advanced combat technique triggered by performing a perfect block after a Critical Hit. This potent attack deals six times the damage of a normal M1 and is indicated by striking black and red particles. It requires exceptional timing and mastery of blocking.

| Mechanic        | Activation                                                                       | Effect                                                                                                    | Damage Multiplier |
| :-------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------- |
| **Critical Hit** | Perfect block on opponent's M1                                                   | Next basic attack deals triple damage. Can last indefinitely until ragdolled.                             | **3x**            |
| **Black Flash**  | Perfect block after a Critical Hit (without being ragdolled)                     | Next basic attack deals double Critical Hit damage (6x normal M1).                                      | **6x**            |

<Callout type="info" title="Black Flash Limitations">
Black Flashes must be performed on a player, not a training dummy. They also have a short window (~4 seconds) after a Critical Hit to be activated.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering Movement and Dashes">
Movement is paramount in The Strongest Battlegrounds. Effective use of dashes allows you to engage, disengage, evade attacks, and position yourself for optimal combos. Understanding the properties and cooldowns of each dash type is crucial for advanced play.

### Types of Dashes

Dashes are executed by pressing **Q** while moving in a specific direction. Each type serves a unique purpose in combat.

<Callout type="tip" title="Side Dash Advantage">
The **side dash** is generally considered the most effective due to its lower cooldown and lack of post-dash vulnerability, making it ideal for advanced combo extensions and evasive maneuvers.
</Callout>

| Dash Type      | Input (PC)      | Cooldown | Range (Tiles) | Key Characteristics                                                                                             |
| :------------- | :-------------- | :------- | :------------ | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Forward Dash** | W + Q           | 5 seconds | 4.5           | Quickly closes distance, can auto-attack for 4% damage. Leaves a brief vulnerability window.                    |
| **Side Dash**    | A or D + Q      | 2 seconds | 3             | Versatile for evasion and combo setup. No vulnerability window. Distance decreases with lower health.           |
| **Back Dash**    | S + Q           | 5 seconds | 4.5           | Covers the most distance, primarily used for escaping wide-area attacks (like Ultimates) or waking up from ragdoll. |

<Callout type="info" title="Ping and Dash Execution">
Side and back dashes are client-handled, meaning they feel instantaneous regardless of ping. Forward dashes are server-handled due to their attack animation, introducing a slight delay based on your ping.
</Callout>

### Ragdoll and Roll States

When hit by certain attacks, you can enter a **ragdoll** or **roll** state.

- **Ragdoll**: You are incapacitated for 2 seconds but can perform a **Ragdoll Cancel** (side or back dash) to recover early and gain brief stun immunity.
- **Roll**: You are knocked back and unable to use evasive moves, with few exceptions. Being hit while already rolling can lead to a longer ragdoll.

### Wall Combos

Utilizing the environment can significantly extend your combo potential. Each character has a unique wall combo that deals additional damage.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Position Your Opponent">

  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Execute the Wall Combo">

  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Follow Up">
    Depending on the character, you may be able to follow up with additional skills or M1s after the wall combo.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Practice Wall Combos">

### Combat Terminology

Familiarizing yourself with common fighting game terms will help you understand advanced discussions and strategies.

| Term        | Definition                                                                               | Importance in TSB                                                                                |
| :---------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Stun**    | A state where a player cannot perform actions after being hit.                             | Crucial for combo extensions; knowing stun duration helps plan next moves.                       |
| **I-frames** | Invincibility frames; periods where a character cannot be damaged or interrupted.         | Essential for defensive maneuvers, escaping combos, and safely executing Ultimates.                |
| **Armor**   | A state where a player cannot be damaged or stunned by regular attacks during an action. | Allows certain moves to power through opponent attacks, but can be broken by specific skills.     |
| **Punish**  | Landing a hit during an opponent's vulnerable state (e.g., after a missed attack).        | Capitalizing on opponent mistakes is a core aspect of competitive play.                          |
