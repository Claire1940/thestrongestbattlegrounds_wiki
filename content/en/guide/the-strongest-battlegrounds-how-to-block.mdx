<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering Perfect Blocks and Critical Hits">

The mechanics of the strongest battlegrounds how to block extend beyond simple damage mitigation. If you time your block perfectly—pressing the button just as the opponent's M1 strike is about to land—you perform a **Perfect Block**. This is signaled by a subtle sound cue and allows you to transition immediately into a **Critical Hit**.

A Critical Hit is an enhanced basic attack that deals triple the damage of a standard M1. This state lasts until you are ragdolled, meaning you can hold onto your "Critical" status to set up a devastating combo. If you manage to perform another Perfect Block while holding a Critical Hit, you unlock the legendary **Black Flash**.

<Section icon={Map} title="Defensive Mobility: Dashing and Cancels">

Knowing how to block is only half the battle; knowing when to dash is the other. Dashing allows you to reposition or escape dangerous situations where blocking would fail (such as against unblockable moves). There are three main types of dashes, each with specific cooldowns and uses.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Side Dashing (The L-Shape)">
    Instead of dashing straight back, use a "Side Dash" (A/D + Q). This allows you to circle around a blocking opponent. If you hit them from behind, their block is disabled, allowing you to start a combo.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Back Dashing for I-Frames">
    Back dashing (S + Q) provides a few frames of invincibility (I-Frames). This is perfect for avoiding rapid-fire attacks or projectiles that would otherwise chip away at your block.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Ragdoll Canceling">
    If you are knocked down, press **Q** to perform a Ragdoll Cancel. This allows you to get up early and potentially dash behind your opponent to "Ragdoll Counter" them.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Dash Type | Cooldown | Distance | Primary Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Side Dash** | 2 Seconds | ~3 Tiles | Flanking and Evasion |
| **Forward Dash** | 5 Seconds | 4.5 Tiles | Combo Starting (Blockable) |
| **Back Dash** | 5 Seconds | 4.5 Tiles | Defensive Reset |

<Callout type="tip" title="Health vs. Distance">
Be aware that your Side Dash distance decreases as your health gets lower. This mechanic prevents players from "running" indefinitely when they are near defeat.
</Callout>

Advanced defense in 2026 involves utilizing **Armor** and **Hyperarmor**. These are states where your character cannot be stunned by regular attacks, effectively acting as an "active block" while you are performing a move.

- **Armor**: You take damage but do not flinch from M1s or most skills.
- **Hyperarmor (I-Frames)**: You take no damage and cannot be interrupted by any means.
